Based on the diagram, building components are divided into the **Substructure** (below ground) and **Superstructure** (above ground).

---

## 1. Substructure (Foundation)

This is the lowest part of the building that transfers loads to the soil.

- **G.L. (Ground Level)**: The natural surface level of the ground.
- **Foundation (Substructure)**: The lowest load-bearing part of the building that transfers loads to the soil.
- **Concrete Bed**: The bottom-most plain cement concrete (PCC) layer placed directly on the soil to provide a level, firm base for the foundation.
- **Width of Foundation / Foundation Depth**: The horizontal spread and vertical depth of the concrete bed, designed to distribute the building's weight safely over the soil.
- **Vertical D.P.C. (Damp Proof Course)**: A vertical water-resistant barrier (usually bituminous) applied on the outer face of the foundation wall to prevent moisture from seeping upward into the superstructure.
- **Plinth**: The masonry projection (or wall) built between the foundation and the ground floor, acting as a transition base.
- **Plinth Level**: The top surface of the plinth, which is usually kept higher than the ground to prevent rainwater entry.
- **Steps**: The flight of stairs built at the entrance to bridge the height difference between the G.L. and the Plinth Level.
- **Plinth Level Floor**: The finished ground floor slab laid at the plinth level.

---

## 2. Superstructure (Main Building)

This is the portion of the building above the plinth level.

### A. Vertical & Horizontal Wall Elements

- **Wall**: The vertical solid or hollow masonry/concrete partition that encloses the space and carries loads.
- **Corbel**: A projecting structural support (like a bracket) built into or projecting from the wall to support a beam, balcony, or overhanging slab above.
- **String Course**: A horizontal, projecting decorative band/molding running along the outside face of the wall to break the monotony and shed rainwater down the sides.
- **Cornice**: A large, horizontal decorative molding projecting from the top of the wall or just below the roof to throw rainwater clear of the walls.
- **Frieze**: The horizontal flat band or decorative panel located directly below the cornice on the wall surface.
- **Blocking Course**: A solid course of masonry laid directly above the cornice, acting as a base to hold the parapet or coping firmly.
- **Parapet Wall**: The low protective wall that extends vertically above the roof slab around the terrace edge.
- **Coping**: The protective capping or covering fixed on top of the parapet wall. It is sloped to shed rainwater away from the wall.

### B. Floors & Roof

- **Ground Floor / First Floor**: The different horizontal levels of the building.
- **R.C.C. Slab**: The reinforced cement concrete horizontal flat structural element used for roofs and floors.
- **First Floor Slab**: The RCC slab separating the ground and first floor levels.
- **Flooring of Terrace**: The top finished surface (tiles, waterproofing layers) laid over the RCC roof slab to make it walkable and watertight.

### C. Openings (Doors & Windows)

- **R.C.C. Lintel**: A reinforced concrete beam placed horizontally over the top of door and window openings to support the weight of the wall above.
- **Door Frame / Window Frame**: The structural wooden, metal, or UPVC framework fixed into the opening to hold the door/window shutters.
- **Sill**: The horizontal bottom horizontal member of a window frame (or the slab/projection beneath it) that supports the window.
- **Reveal**: The vertical side faces or jambs of the opening within the wall thickness, connecting the frame to the outer wall surface.
- **Weathershed** (appears twice): The sloped projection or groove provided over/under windows to throw rainwater away from the wall and window openings.

### D. Miscellaneous Term

- **Toothing**: A brickwork term referring to the projecting bricks left alternately at the end of a masonry wall. This provides a "tooth" pattern to allow a future extension of the wall to bond tightly with the existing one.
